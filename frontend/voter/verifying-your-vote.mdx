---
title: "Verifying Your Vote"
description: "Use your 12-character N2 code after the election closes to confirm your ballot appears in published results and carries a valid cryptographic signature."
---

Evoting supports public verifiability: once the election ends, all cast ballots are published as a list of `(N2, vote)` pairs. Any person who holds an N2 code can check whether the corresponding ballot appears in that list and whether its cryptographic signature is intact.

## Prerequisites

Vote verification is only available after the election moves to the `vote_ended` phase. If voting is still in progress, the results have not been published yet.

You need:

- Your **N2 code** from registration. N2 codes are exactly 12 uppercase letters and numbers (for example, `A1B2C3D4E5F6`).
- The election to be in the `vote_ended` phase.

## Steps

**1. Navigate to the verification page**

Go to `/results/verify-vote`. You will see a form with a single input field.

**2. Enter your N2 code**

Type or paste your N2 code exactly as you received it during registration.

**3. Submit and review the result**

Click **Verify**. The system sends your N2 code to `POST /results/verify-vote` and returns a verification record.

## Understanding the response

The API returns a `VerifyVoteResponse` object:

```ts
type VerifyVoteResponse = {
  found: boolean;
  status: "valid" | "invalid_signature" | "invalid_n2" | null;
  vote: string | null;
  message: string;
};
```

**valid** — your ballot was found in the published results and its cryptographic signature passed verification. Your vote was counted as cast.

**invalid_signature** — a ballot with your N2 code was found, but the signature check failed. This may indicate the ballot was tampered with after submission.

**invalid_n2** — the N2 hash in the published record does not match the N2 you provided. The ballot associated with this record may have been forged.

**found: false** — no ballot with your N2 code appears in the published results. Your vote was not recorded in the final tally.

## Response fields

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `found` | `boolean` | Whether an entry for your N2 code exists in the published results |
| `status` | `string \| null` | Cryptographic verification outcome. `null` when `found` is `false` |
| `vote` | `string \| null` | The candidate recorded against your N2 code, if found |
| `message` | `string` | Human-readable description of the outcome |

## Public and anonymous verification

Verification is completely public and anonymous. Anyone who has an N2 code can verify the corresponding ballot — you do not need to log in or prove ownership. This allows independent auditors to verify the entire election by checking every published N2 against the results.
